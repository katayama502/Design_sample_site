Curriculum

Core Program

01 — Foundation

Geometric Mastery

Begin with the circle, square, and triangle. Every structure in the built world resolves to these three elemental forms. Master them before attempting complexity.

02 — Color Theory

Primary Logic

Red carries action. Blue carries thought. Yellow carries spirit. Learn to assign color with purpose, never decoration. The palette is your vocabulary, not your ornament.

03 — Composition

Grid Discipline

The grid is not a constraint — it is the skeleton of clarity. Through strict modular composition, learn to create visual hierarchy that communicates before it pleases.

04 — Workshop

Material Making

Design and craft are inseparable. Students work in wood, steel, and ceramic — understanding how material nature demands honest expression, not concealment.

Philosophy

Our Principles

Form Follows Function

Every visual decision must justify itself through purpose. If removing an element leaves the work cleaner without loss of meaning, remove it. Decoration is failure.

Unity of Art and Craft

There is no hierarchy between fine art and functional craft. The chair is as worthy of mastery as the painting. Great design lives in both the gallery and the home.

Elemental Reduction

Strip all design to its irreducible core. The circle, the square, the triangle are not simplifications — they are perfections. Complexity is a problem to be solved, not a virtue to be celebrated.

Design for All

Great design should not be reserved for the wealthy. Industrial production and honest material usage bring beauty to every household. This is the moral imperative of the designer.
